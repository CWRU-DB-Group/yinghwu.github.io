Bibliography

 Dr. Yinghui Wu is an assistant professor at the School of EECS, Washington State University.  He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Edinburgh, UK in 2010, and a B.S. degree in Computer Science from Peking University in 2007. Before he joined WSU,  he was a research scientist at Department of Computer Science, University of California Santa Barbara, and served as a member of Network Science Collaborative Technique Alliance (NS-CTA). His research interests are in areas of  big data,  databases, data mining and data analytics over complex data, including  graph databases, graph analytics and network science, knowledge graphs, social/information network analytics and network security. His current research projects are on cost-effective and scalable information systems with adaptive performance and high usability, over knowledge graphs and  knowledge bases.
