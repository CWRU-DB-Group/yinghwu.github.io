Bio

Yinghui Wu is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer and Data Sciences, Case School of Engineering in the Case Western Reserve University. Yinghui works in data management and analytics with emphasis on knowledge extraction and exploration from complex and big data. The CWRU database group devises and designs novel data management and analysis paradigms and tools to support data and knowledge exploration in Big Data era. His research has received several awards including ACM SIGMOD research highlight award (2018), SIGMOD Best Paper Award (2017), VLDB Best Demo (2017), Google Faculty Research Award (2015), and ICDE Best Paper runner-up (2014). He has also received a VLDB Distinguished Reviewer award (2019). His current research has a focus on cost-effective, scalable data systems for knowledge and data exploration. His research is currently supported by NSF, DOE/PNNL, USDA and Siemens.

Yinghui received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Edinburgh, UK, and B.S. in Computer Science from Peking University, China.  He currently holds joint position as staff scientist at Pacific Northwest National Lab and faculty at Washington State University. He held the positions of research scientist at Department of Computer Science, University of California Santa Barbara, and visiting scholars at Army Research Lab and Raytheon BBN Technologies. He served as a member of Network Science Collaborative Technique Alliance (NS-CTA).
