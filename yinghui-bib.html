Bio

Yinghui Wu is a Theodore L. and Dana J.Schroeder Associate Professor in the Department of Computer and Data Sciences, Case School of Engineering in the Case Western Reserve University. He holds a joint position as a staff scientist at the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory.  His area is in Data and Knowledge management, Databases, Data Mining and Machine Learning, with a recent focus on graph data management, graph learning and analytics.

Yinghui directs the CWRU database group. His group devises and designs novel data management and analysis paradigms and tools to support data and knowledge exploration in Big Data. He co-directs the Materials Data Science for Stockpile Stewardship Center of Excellence (MDS3 COE) with Dr. Roger French. His research has received several awards including an ACM SIGMOD research highlight award, several Best Paper and Runner Up Awards (SIGMOD, VLDB, ICDE, IEEE BigData), and Google Faculty Research Award. He has received a VLDB Distinguished Reviewer award. His current research has a focus on cost-effective, scalable data systems for knowledge and data exploration. His research is supported by NSF, DOE, USDA, national laboratories and industry partners.

Yinghui received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Edinburgh, UK, and B.S. in Computer Science from Peking University, China.  He held the positions of research scientist at Department of Computer Science, University of California Santa Barbara, and visiting scholars at Army Research Lab and Raytheon BBN Technologies. He served as a member of Network Science Collaborative Technique Alliance (NS-CTA). He serves as an associate editor for ACM Journal of Data and Information Quality and a guest editor for WWW Journal.
