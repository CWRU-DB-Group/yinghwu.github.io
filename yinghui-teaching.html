  • CPT_S 415 Big Data (Fall 16-18 Tu/Th 4:15-5:30 pm)

    This course introduces basic technology (algorithms, architectures, systems) and advanced research topics in connection with large-scale data management and information extraction techniques for big data. The course will start by introducing Big data models, databases and query languages, and cover modern distributed database systems and algorithms and Big data systems adopted in industry and science applications. Implementation of a distributed database on a standalone machine will be covered and students will learn how to build their own data analytical systems for big data. Distributed storage and parallel processing and architectures that support data analytics will be examined, and students will learn how to implement a distributed data processing system. The course will also cover critical topics in mining and knowledge discovery of big data, with applications in social analytics, cyber security, and information networks, among others that are already in public eye.

  • Advanced Database (Fall 17, Spring 18,16 Tu/Th 9:15-10:30 am)

    This course is an advanced study of database theory and systems, and advanced research topics in connection with large-scale data management and information extraction techniques for big data.  We will introduce modern noSQL and newSQL databases, query languages, and systems adopted in industry and science applications. We then study the internals of database management systems (indexing, query optimization, data consistency), and application-driven databases including distributed/parallel databases, in-memory databases, stream processing, graph databases. The course will also cover critical topics in knowledge discovery of big data (data quality, data mining, data privacy), with applications in in social analytics, cyber security, and information networks.
